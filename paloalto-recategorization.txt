ori-bio.app is an open-source, web-based molecular biology platform for visualizing and analyzing DNA plasmid sequences — a research tool for molecular biologists, graduate students, and postdoctoral researchers. It is actively used by academic researchers at Washington University in St. Louis.

The domain was registered in May 2026 and is hosted on Vercel (a major cloud platform). The site is a legitimate scientific research application, not spam, malware, or a domain generation algorithm product. It is being blocked solely due to the Newly-Registered-Domain classification, which is a false positive for this established research tool.

The site's source code is publicly available on GitHub (github.com/shandley/ori-bio). Vercel confirms the domain has a valid configuration serving a Next.js application.
